## The Confusion

Walk into any hosiery section — physical or digital — and you'll see "stay-ups," "thigh-highs," and "stockings" used almost interchangeably. They're not the same thing. The differences matter for comfort, for aesthetics, and for whether you spend your evening adjusting or enjoying.

Let's sort this out once and for all.

## Stay-Ups (Self-Supporting Stockings)

**What they are:** Stockings with a built-in silicone grip band at the top. They hold themselves up without any additional hardware.

**The grip band** is the defining feature. It's a strip of silicone (sometimes in dots, sometimes in solid lines) applied to the inside of the welt — the decorative top band. The silicone grips your skin through friction.

**Pros:**
Nothing to wear underneath or over. Put them on, they stay. No clips, no belt, no fuss. The silicone band is invisible under most clothing. Modern stay-ups from quality brands (Wolford, Falke, Fogal) hold reliably for 8+ hours.

**Cons:**
Heat, sweat, and body lotion can weaken the silicone grip. If you have very slim thighs, some stay-ups may slide. The silicone band can feel warm in summer. Over many washes, the silicone loses tackiness.

**Best for:** Daily wear, office, evenings out, anyone who values simplicity.

## Thigh-Highs (The Ambiguous Term)

Here's where the confusion lives. "Thigh-high" is a **length description**, not a type. It simply means "a stocking that reaches the thigh." Both stay-ups and garter stockings can be thigh-highs.

In common usage — especially in the US — "thigh-highs" usually means stay-ups. In European hosiery, the term is used less often. When shopping, always check whether the product has a silicone band (stay-up) or a plain welt (garter stocking).

**The rule:** If someone says "thigh-highs" without further specification, they probably mean stay-ups.

## Garter Stockings (Suspender Stockings)

**What they are:** Stockings with a plain top welt (no silicone) designed to be held up by a garter belt (also called a suspender belt). The belt clips onto the stocking top with metal or plastic fasteners.

**The garter belt** sits at the waist or hip and has 4–6 adjustable straps that clip onto the stocking welts. This is the oldest hosiery system — predating silicone technology by a century.

**Pros:**
Zero slip risk when properly fitted. The straps create a visual line that many find aesthetically striking. No silicone touching skin (better for sensitive skin or hot weather). The stockings themselves last longer because there's no silicone to degrade.

**Cons:**
Extra piece to wear (the belt). Fastening clips can show through tight clothing. Requires practice to attach smoothly. The belt can shift during movement if sized wrong.

**Best for:** Special occasions, boudoir, anyone who prefers the classic look, sensitive skin.

## The Comparison

| Feature | Stay-Ups | Garter Stockings |
|---|---|---|
| Stays up via | Silicone band | Garter belt + clips |
| Ease of use | Very easy | Moderate learning curve |
| Visibility under clothes | Minimal | Belt/clips may show |
| Durability | Silicone degrades over time | Stockings last longer |
| Comfort in heat | Can feel warm | Better airflow |
| Aesthetic | Clean, modern | Classic, dramatic |
| Daily wearability | Excellent | Possible but more effort |

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose stay-ups if:** You want convenience above all. You wear hosiery daily or semi-regularly. You prefer a sleek, nothing-extra approach.

**Choose garter stockings if:** You enjoy the ritual of dressing. You want the visual impact of straps and clips. You have sensitive skin that reacts to silicone. You're dressing for an occasion that calls for classic elegance.

**Choose both if:** You're a hosiery enthusiast. Different tools, different moods.

## Care Tip

Both types benefit from hand washing in cool water with mild detergent. For stay-ups specifically: avoid getting body lotion, oil, or perfume on the silicone band — these break down the grip faster than anything else. Dry flat, never wring.
