## Why Care Matters

A €35 pair of Wolfords that lasts 30+ wears costs less per use than a €5 pair that ladders on day three. But that longevity is not automatic — it depends entirely on how you treat them between wears. The difference between careless and careful handling is often the difference between 5 wears and 50.

This is not about being precious. It is about being practical. A simple care routine takes less than five minutes and pays for itself many times over.

## The Washing Rule

Hand wash. Always. Every time. No exceptions.

A washing machine — even on a delicate cycle, even in a mesh bag — creates friction that degrades nylon fibres at the molecular level. You cannot see the damage immediately, but after three machine washes, the material has already lost structural integrity. That mysterious run that appeared "from nowhere" almost certainly started in the drum.

The process is simple: lukewarm water, a drop of pH-neutral soap or specialist hosiery wash, gentle squeeze — never twist or wring. Rinse in cool water. Press between two towels to remove excess water. Lay flat or drape over a towel rack to air dry.

Total time: four minutes. Cost: essentially zero.

## The Drying Truth

Never use a radiator, hair dryer, or direct sunlight. Heat is nylon's enemy. It weakens elastane fibres and causes silicone bands to degrade — which is why stay-ups that once gripped perfectly start sliding after being dried on a heated rack.

Room temperature air drying is the only safe method. In winter, a well-ventilated bathroom works perfectly. Most stay-ups dry completely within 4-6 hours at room temperature.

## Storage That Matters

The drawer tangle is the silent killer of hosiery. Stockings and stay-ups stuffed into a shared drawer catch on each other, on zippers, on rough wood edges. Every snag is a future ladder.

The solution does not require anything elaborate. Roll each pair individually — one stocking nested inside the other, then rolled from toe to band. Store rolled pairs in a soft-lined drawer divider or even a simple fabric pouch.

Some enthusiasts use individual zip-lock bags, which also works and has the added benefit of keeping pairs together and visible. The method matters less than the principle: each pair should be separated and protected from friction.

## The Putting-On Ritual

More hosiery is destroyed in the act of putting it on than in an entire day of wearing it. Rings, rough cuticles, dry skin on heels — these are the real enemies.

Before putting on any fine denier hosiery:

Remove all rings and bracelets. Moisturise hands if they are dry. Gather the stocking from toe to band — never pull from the top. Slide over the foot, then gently unroll up the leg. Adjust the silicone band last, ensuring it sits flat against clean, dry skin.

The gathering technique is essential. Pulling a stocking up by the top creates concentrated stress at a single point. Gathering distributes the force across the entire length and is how hosiery professionals handle the material.

## A Note on Nails

If you regularly wear fine denier hosiery — anything under 20D — consider keeping your fingernails smooth rather than pointed. A single rough nail edge can create a micro-tear that becomes a full ladder within hours. A quick pass with a glass nail file before putting on your stockings takes ten seconds and prevents the most common cause of premature runs.

## The Reward

Proper care is not a chore — it is an extension of the ritual that makes hosiery worth wearing in the first place. The moment you slide on a perfectly maintained stay-up and feel it grip exactly where it should, with the same tension and sheerness as the day you bought it — that is the reward. And it costs nothing but a few minutes of attention.
